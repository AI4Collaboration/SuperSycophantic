\section{Related Work}\label{sec:related-work}
\textbf{Sycophancy in Psychology.}
Research on social conformity has long demonstrated that individuals systematically
adjust their judgments under group consensus systematically \cite{asch1956studies}, driven by informational and normative influence \cite{deutsch1955study}. Two parallel 
theoretical traditions have elaborated on the mechanisms of normative influence. Goffman~\cite{goffman1955facework} established that social interactions are governed by face 
preservation, while Jones~\cite{jones1964ingratiation} formalized 
normative influence as a reward strategy through the ingratiation 
framework, which consists of three primary tactics: other-enhancement, opinion conformity, and self-presentation. This framework has been extensively applied in the study of sycophancy in organizational settings \cite{higgins2003influence, higgins2004applicant, westphal2006boardroom}. Cialdini~\cite{cialdini1984influence} 
further distilled social influence research into six principles: reciprocity, 
commitment, social proof, liking, authority, and scarcity. Despite the richness of 
this field, existing sycophancy benchmarks have drawn on it only partially.

\textbf{Sycophancy in LLMs.}
Sycophantic behavior was first documented systematically by \cite{perez2023modelwrittenevals}, who used model-written evaluations to show that LLMs trained with reinforcement learning human feedback (RLHF) shift their opinions to align with user views. Various mitigation strategies have since been proposed, such as the use of synthetic data \cite{wei2023syntheticsycophancy}, direct preference optimization \cite{khan2024mitigating}, and pinpoint tuning \cite{chen2024yes} have been shown to reduce this behavior \cite{malmqvist2024sycophancymitigations}. On the evaluation side, \cite{sharma2023towards} and \cite{SycEval} introduced more realistic open-ended evaluation tasks with \textsc{SycophancyEval} and \textsc{SycEval}, and the scope of the evaluation has since expanded to domain-specific settings such as mathematical theorem proving \cite{BROKENMATH}, medicine \cite{chen2025helpfulness}, political opinion \cite{GermanPartiesQA} and broader robustness testing \cite{PARROT, BEACON, DARKBENCH, duffy2024sycobench}, although all remain confined to single-turn interactions. As sycophancy in practice often unfolds across dialog turns, subsequent benchmarks such as \textsc{SYCON Bench} have examined the multi-turn setting \cite{TRUTHDECAY, FLIPFLOP, SYCONBENCH}. Beyond explicit change in opinion, \cite{ELEPHANT} introduce \textsc{ELEPHANT} which is grounded in social psychological theory to capture more implicit forms of sycophantic behavior. This work brings together the multi-turn pressure dimension, grounded social dimensions of sycophancy, and the role of user influence as a trigger into a unified evaluation framework.

\textbf{Mechanistic Understanding of LLM Sycophancy.}
Several previous studies have shown that sycophancy is encoded in a linear structure within the activation space \cite{jain2025psychometrictraits, papadatos2024probepenalty, chen2025personavectors, rimsky2024steering, templeton2024scaling, vennemeyer2025causalsycophancy, genadi2026linear}. However, much of this literature has implicitly treated behavior as a single phenomenon \cite{rimsky2024steering, chen2025personavectors, papadatos2024probepenalty}, yet recent findings reveal that behavior decomposes into multiple distinct directions. \cite{vennemeyer2025causalsycophancy} showed that the sycophantic agreement and sycophantic praise occupy a separable subspace that can be amplified or suppressed independently of the other. In addition, complementary analysis through sparse autoencoders finds that sycophancy-related behaviors correspond to distinct, non-overlapping features in the residual stream \cite{templeton2024scaling}. The conditions that trigger each subtype are less understood. As a first step, \cite{wang2025truthoverridden}, shows that different prompt formulations induce different degrees of sycophantic expression. Together, these findings motivate an evaluation framework that goes beyond measuring sycophancy as a single score, instead systematically varying triggers to elicit distinct subtypes of sycophantic behavior.

\textbf{Sycophancy in Chain-of-Thought Reasoning}
Although sycophancy research has focused largely on final outputs,
a growing body of work has shown that sycophancy signals cause models to construct post-hoc rationalizations rather than faithfully reasoning towards correct answers \cite{turpin2023languagemodels, turpin2023language, chang2026thermodynamic, walden2026reasoning, chua2025reasoning}. These influences remain largely not verbalized \cite{chua2025reasoning}, and correct reasoning can precede sycophantic final answers \cite{chang2026thermodynamic}. Mechanistic work has also revealed that sycophantic commitment is detectable before thinking tokens are generated, but evolves throughout the reasoning trace\cite{feng2026good, mirtaheri2026catching, duszenko2026anchors, hu2026MONICA}. In this work, we examine how sycophancy signals corrupt chain-of-thought reasoning across multi-turn pressure trajectories, analyzing both reasoning trace dynamics and post-hoc self-justification after capitulation.
